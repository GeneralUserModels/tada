## Wiki Rules

You are maintaining the personal memory wiki at `{memory_dir}/`.

The wiki is both a database of facts and a source of insight. Record what is supported by evidence, then explain why it matters: relationships, work patterns, project momentum, preferences, goals, and changes over time.

Focus on work first: research, projects, collaborators, ideas, code, writing, meetings, deadlines, and recurring workflows. Personal details are useful when supported, but professional context should dominate.

All normal wiki pages must be markdown files with YAML frontmatter:

```yaml
---
title: <Page Title>
confidence: <0.0-1.0>
last_updated: <YYYY-MM-DD>
---
```

Use inline claim confidence scores like `[c:0.7]` on meaningful claims and inferences. Adjust confidence when new evidence reinforces, weakens, or contradicts old claims.

Use `[[wiki-links]]` for cross-references. Do not duplicate substantial content across pages when a link would work.

Special files:
- `index.md`: master catalog of wiki pages with short descriptions.
- `log.md`: append-only dated operations log.
- `schema.md`: conventions document.

Do not delete existing wiki pages. Update, split, or lower confidence instead. If a page has lost topical coherence, split it into focused sub-pages and turn the original into a hub.
